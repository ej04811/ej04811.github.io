1.       Provide safe, quality, holistic, evidence-based patient-centered care in a variety of healthcare settings to diverse patient populations across the lifespan.

a.       The nurse should always be up today on current evidence based practice guidelines and Implement those guidelines in a safe manner, making sure to provide for patient safety and satisfaction. The nurse can accomplish this by continuing to pursue a new training and Reading literature our new or improved techniques relevant to the settings that the nurse is working in. whether that setting is in an acute, long-term, or a non bedside area of Nursing.

b.       I have accomplished this first by attending multiple clinical sites and simulation training throughout the nursing program. I have also completed multiple third-party training such as those offered by the CDC and through the Hurst review process.

c.       Caring Paper

d.       Japan

2.      Use critical thinking and clinical reasoning to make patient-centered care decisions

a.       The nurse should always be using his / her judgment when caring for patients. Every situation is different and the nurse needs to realize this and be able to assess the situation and make adjustments to care according to that assessment. The nurse should never solely rely on what they have been told by other caregivers either in writing or  Verbally.

b.      Maternity Final evaluation

c.      Nursing Immersion Final evaluation

3.      Implement quality improvement strategies within a variety of healthcare systems.

a.       The nurse should try different evidence-based practices to see what will be the best fit at their facility. they should seek to improve upon current practices wherever they see a need.

b.      Hypertension

c.      QI Paper

4.      Collaborate and communicate with patients, health-care professionals and members of community groups to improve health outcomes

a.       The nurse needs to work with patients, healthcare workers, and the community in efforts to improve Healthcare outcomes at the individual and Community levels.

b.      Lung Cancer

c.      QI Project

5.      Use information and technology to communicate, manage knowledge, enhance safety and decision making, and coordinate care.

a.       The nurse needs to be proficient with modern technology and they need to use that knowledge in all aspects of patient care from communicating / collaborating with other members of the team 2 using information an electronic records to make informed patient care decisions.

b.      MRSA

c.      CAUTI

6.      Engage in leadership/management of care activities to improve direct and indirect patient care within a variety of healthcare systems.

a.       The nurse should gain experience in management of individual patient care and in higher level management such as acting as a charge nurse in multiple settings. The nurse should be ready to take on leadership roles outside of direct patient care.

b.      Change

c.      QI Project

7.      Assimilate professional, legal, and ethical guidelines in practice as a professional nurse.

a.       A nurse should be able to translate guidelines on legal, ethical, and professional  issues into their daily practice of caring for patients at the bedside and in other aspects of nursing. They should be aware of these principles in all aspects of the nursing care that they provide.

b.      Civility

c.      NIH
